# Brand Identity: Skill Stack

*Last updated: December 2024*

---

## Brand Persona

**Name:** Skill Stack
**Role:** Creator's AI skills curator and teacher
**Background:** Born from production work at a major publisher and podcast network. Real skills, battle-tested on daily deadlines. Not theory - proven systems that ship content.

**Personality Traits:**
- **Practical over theoretical** - Every skill has been used in real production
- **Direct but warm** - Cuts to the point without being cold
- **Confidently helpful** - Knows what works, eager to share it

**Core Beliefs:**
- Skills compound. One well-crafted prompt creates leverage across everything you make.
- The industry keeps rebuilding the "OS" (custom agents). The opportunity is in "applications" (skills).
- Creators shouldn't need to study AI tactics. They should focus on their craft.

**Superpower:** Production-tested skills with wizard-guided setup. "Unzip one file. Let it unfold into something beautiful."

---

## Target Audience

### Primary Audience

**Demographics:** Content creators, solopreneurs, small business owners. Ages 30-55. Creating newsletters, podcasts, or social content.

**Psychographics:** Craft-focused. Know AI can help but overwhelmed by options. Value their time. Want results, not experiments.

**Current State:**
- Spending too much time on AI prompt engineering
- Content doesn't sound like them
- Frustrated by generic AI output
- Know there's a better way but don't have time to find it

**Desired State:**
- AI as invisible amplifier
- Consistent voice across all content
- More output in less time
- Focus on ideas, not mechanics

**How They Describe It:**
- "AI makes everything sound the same"
- "I don't have time to learn all this"
- "I just want it to sound like me"

### Secondary Audiences

**Ghostwriters & Agencies:** Need to capture and replicate client voices
**Content Teams:** Need brand consistency across multiple writers

## Voice & Tone

### Voice Spectrum

| Dimension | Rating | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Formal/Casual | 4 | Conversational, not corporate |
| Expert/Peer | 3 | Knowledgeable friend, not lecturer |
| Serious/Playful | 3 | Mostly serious, occasional wit |
| Reserved/Opinionated | 4 | Has a point of view |
| Abstract/Concrete | 5 | Always concrete, specific |

### Sentence Patterns

**Average length:** Mixed - short punchy statements, occasional longer explanations
**Variation:** Rhythm through variety. Short for emphasis. Longer for explanation.
**Signature structures:**
- Direct statements: "Skills compound."
- The pivot: "Not X. Y."
- The benefit first: "Get [result]. Here's how."

### Word Choice

**Vocabulary:** Simple to moderate. Plain language.
**Contractions:** Always - sounds more natural
**Jargon:** Explain or avoid. Never assume knowledge.
**Informality:** Casual but not sloppy

### Anti-Patterns (NEVER do)

- ❌ Corporate speak ("leverage," "synergy," "optimize")
- ❌ AI hype language ("revolutionary," "game-changing")
- ❌ Vague promises ("boost your productivity")
- ❌ Academic distance ("one might consider")
- ❌ Exclamation points in body text

### Signature Phrases

- "Skills > Agents"
- "Unzip one file. Let it unfold."
- "Define once, use everywhere."
- "Production-tested, not theoretical."

## Values & Mission

### Mission

Help creators leverage AI without losing their voice or wasting time on prompt engineering.

### Core Values

1. **Practical over theoretical** - Every skill we share has been used in real production
2. **Voice preservation** - AI should amplify your voice, not replace it
3. **Time respect** - We value your time. Setup should be minutes, not hours.

### What We Stand For

- Skills as composable building blocks
- Human voice over AI slop
- Creators keeping their distinctive edge

### What We Oppose

- AI hype and empty promises
- Generic, homogenized content
- Complexity for its own sake
- "Just use ChatGPT" without guidance
