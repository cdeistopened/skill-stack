# Checkpoint 3 Template: YouTube Strategy

This template demonstrates the final output format for Checkpoint 3: clean, actionable specifications for YouTube publishing and social media promotion.

---

## WHAT CHECKPOINT 3 DELIVERS

A single document containing:
1. **YouTube Title** (with guest name for credibility)
2. **YouTube Thumbnail Spec** (simple visual + 2-4 words max)
3. **YouTube Description** (opening hook + full description + resources + chapters)
4. **Cold Open Script** (verbatim clips with speaker labels)
5. **Approved Social Clips** (3 clips with on-screen hooks, ready to post)
6. **Chapter Breakdown** (5-10 words per chapter, keyword-rich, compelling)

---

## CHECKPOINT 3 OUTPUT FORMAT

### Title
**[Guest Name]: [Bold Claim or Statistic] ([Surprising Element])**

**Guidelines:**
- Include guest name for authority
- Lead with strongest hook (stat, question, or bold claim)
- 60-70 characters (optimized for YouTube search)
- Include 1-2 keywords relevant to your topic

---

### Thumbnail Spec

**Visual:** [Key number or stat] | [Supporting visual element] | [Background color]

**Text:** [2-4 WORDS MAX]

**Guidelines:**
- Describe the visual simply (no technical specs)
- Text: 2-4 words maximum
- Avoid redundancy with title (thumbnail and title should complement, not repeat)
- Make thumbnail readable on mobile (assume 280px width)

---

### YouTube Description

**Structure:**
1. Opening hook sentence (1 sentence max)
2. Full description (2-3 sentences)
3. Resources section (with clickable links)
4. Chapters section (formatted timestamps)

**Example:**

[Opening hook that creates curiosity or addresses a pain point.]

In this episode, [Guest credentials] reveals [key insight]. [They/He/She] breaks down [topic area], challenges [popular belief], and explains [core framework or methodology].

**Resources:**
- Guest Website: [URL]
- Book/Product: [URL]
- Guest Social: [URL]

**Chapters:**

(12:15) - [Chapter Title - Key Insight]

---

### Chapter Title Guidelines

**Format:** `(MM:SS) - Descriptive Chapter Title`

**Rules:**
- 5-10 words maximum per title
- Keyword-rich for YouTube SEO
- Curiosity-driven (make each chapter feel like a mini-hook)
- Focus on insights, transformations, key stories - not generic descriptions

**Bad examples:**
- (12:25) - Discussion about the topic
- (29:08) - Guest talks about their experience
- (32:48) - Interview continues

**Good examples:**
- (12:15) - How [Topic] Changed: The [Dramatic Shift]
- (33:00) - The [Popular Belief] Myth Debunked
- (52:30) - [Subject]: The Direct Cause of [Problem]

### Cold Open Script

**Format:** Simple verbatim clips with speaker labels and timestamps

**Example:**

```
(00:00-00:05)
GUEST: [Opening hook statement that grabs attention]

(00:05-00:13)
GUEST: [Building tension with data or surprising insight]

(00:13-00:15)
GUEST: [Strong declarative statement]

(00:15-00:28)
HOST: [Setup question or reaction]

GUEST: [Cliffhanger revelation - STOP HERE]
```

**Guidelines:**
- Total duration: 22-35 seconds
- All quotes must be verbatim (exactly as spoken)
- End with unresolved cliffhanger (listener wants to know more)
- Include speaker labels and approximate timestamps
- Arrange clips in narrative sequence (not chronological - for impact)

The following sections are guidelines for creating Checkpoint 3 - do NOT include quality gates, decision points, or meta-commentary in the actual deliverable. The Checkpoint 3 output should be a clean, actionable document ready for video production handoff.
