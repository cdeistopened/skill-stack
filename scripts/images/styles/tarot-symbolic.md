# Tarot / Symbolic Style

Mystical, archetypal imagery combining medieval woodcut aesthetics with esoteric symbolism.

## Visual Characteristics

- **Line quality**: Bold outlines, confident strokes, woodcut-inspired
- **Symbolism**: Heavy use of archetypal imagery (hands, eyes, celestial bodies)
- **Composition**: Centered, iconic, often symmetrical
- **Color**: Limited palette - often black/white/gold, or black + one symbolic color
- **Borders**: Decorative frames common, often with symbolic elements
- **Flatness**: Minimal perspective, iconic/hieratic presentation

## Historical References

- Rider-Waite tarot deck
- Medieval woodcuts and playing cards
- Alchemical illustrations
- Astrological charts
- Religious iconography (Byzantine, medieval)
- Art Deco mysticism

## Prompt Language

```
STYLE: Tarot card illustration. Bold black outlines with limited color palette.
Symbolic, archetypal imagery presented with hieratic flatness. Medieval woodcut meets
mystical symbolism. Centered, iconic composition with decorative border.
The visual weight and presence of a major arcana card.

TEXTURE: Woodcut-inspired lines, slightly rough edges. Gold or metallic accents optional.
Aged paper or parchment background.

AVOID: Photorealism, 3D perspective, busy backgrounds, cute or casual rendering.
```

## Best For

- Conceptual/philosophical content
- Transformation and process themes
- Archetypal subjects (the writer, the editor, the reader)
- Content about frameworks and systems
- Mystical/creative process topics

## Symbolic Elements

- Hands (creation, craft, guidance)
- Eyes (insight, awareness)
- Stars/moons/suns (illumination, cycles)
- Keys (unlocking, access)
- Flames (transformation, passion)
- Books/scrolls (knowledge, transmission)
